1. Commutative Property

Addition: Matrix addition is commutative. If you add two matrices of the same dimension, the order does not matter.

$A + B = B + A$

Multiplication: Matrix multiplication is generally NOT commutative. Changing the order changes the result entirely (or makes it impossible due to dimension mismatches).

$A \cdot B \neq B \cdot A$

2. Associative Property

Both matrix addition and multiplication follow the associative laws, provided the matrix dimensions align correctly for operations.

$(A + B) + C = A + (B + C)$
$(A \cdot B) \cdot C = A \cdot (B \cdot C)$

3. Distributive Property

Matrix multiplication distributes over matrix addition seamlessly.

$A \cdot (B + C) = A \cdot B + A \cdot C$

4. Transpose of a Matrix

The transpose of a matrix $A$ (denoted as $A^T$ or $A'$) is formed by swapping its rows with its columns. If $A$ is an $m \times n$ matrix, $A^T$ becomes an $n \times m$ matrix.

If $A = \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix}$, then $A^T = \begin{bmatrix} a & c \\ b & d \end{bmatrix}$

5. Multiplication using Transpose

When taking the transpose of a product of two or more matrices, the order of multiplication reverses. This is known as the Reversal Law.

$(A \cdot B)^T = B^T \cdot A^T$

For three matrices, it scales naturally: $(A \cdot B \cdot C)^T = C^T \cdot B^T \cdot A^T$.
